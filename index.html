  • "I loved and adored college, but I had to quit because I couldn’t afford it, so I never really had a chance to experience it. I’m so proud of the kids that, after going through twelve frickin years of school, want to continue. And a lot of these kids are really far from home, so a mama’s hug, or a mama’s love, it don’t even take much but it can make a big difference. So when they come by for food, I don’t know if they ate that day or not, I don’t know if their classes are brutal, all I’m thinking about is making sure this student has a good meal. I refuse to let one child go hungry on my watch."
    - "Mama" Tracey Riley, has worked at the University of Rochester for 8 years.
